The Nobel Prize is one of the most prestigious international awards, given annually to individuals and organizations that have made outstanding contributions to humanity. It was established by Alfred Nobel, a Swedish inventor and philanthropist, through his will in 1895. The first prizes were awarded in 1901. The prize recognizes achievements in science, literature, peace, and economics. The selection process involves nominations from experts in each field, followed by a thorough review by specialized committees. Winners, known as Nobel Laureates, receive a gold medal, a diploma, and a cash prize. The awards are presented every year on December 10, the anniversary of Alfred Nobelâ€™s death. The Nobel Peace Prize is awarded in Oslo, Norway, while all other prizes are presented in Stockholm, Sweden. Some of the most famous Nobel Laureates include Albert Einstein, Marie Curie, Mother Teresa, and Malala Yousafzai. The Nobel Prize remains a symbol of excellence and innovation, inspiring people worldwide to contribute to the progress of humanity.
